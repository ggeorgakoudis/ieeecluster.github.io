The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK)

CLC's SGI Linux Cluster:

The Center for Large-Scale Computation (CLC) in Hong Kong is currently located at The Chinese University, which is a high-tech startup company. The CLC will work on commercially targeted projects as well as collaborating with The Chinese University on academic research that requires high performance computing. As the first phase of the CLC supercluster, it has installed a 32-processor Linux cluster based on the SGI 1450 server powered by the Intel Pentium III Xeon 700 MHz processor. The supercluster will grow to 128 processors in one year. Leveraging SGI's unrivalled experience in supercomputing and scientific computing, the CLC is developing applied capabilities in large-scale cluster computing and to promote cluster technologies for commercial, industrial and scientific use in the region. The supercluster will enable large-scale applications in the community and serve as an R&D facility for the development of optimal turn-key computational solutions to enterprises in the region.

Information Technology Services Center (ITSC):

In 1995, the High Performance Services Team is set up in order to serve the upcoming demand of cluster computing. The team approves requests on using computer clusters, and distributes the computer resources effectively.

The role of ITSC is to provide HPC services to the research users in CU. Their main studies are on the application and resources management/scheduling for HPC clusters/servers. A 10-node Alpha-based cluster has been built to support computation-intensive applications. The cluster includes one DS10, eight DS10Ls, and two XP1000 compute nodes. The total aggregate computation power is 10.4 GFLOPs. The OS used is the Compaq Tru64 UNIX 5.1. Parallel software environment is based on Compaq Extended Math Library version 3.6. The Compaq MPI library (MPICH 1.1) is supported. The PBS (Portable Batch System) and Maui Scheduler have been installed to assist the job submission and management.

The ITSC also has a SUN HPC 6500 server, called data-intensive computing server. This server is equipped with ten UltraSparc II 336 MHz processors and installed with 4 GB main memory. Its theoretical computation performance is 6.72 GFLOPS. This system is intended for research projects that require very fast I/O throughput and huge computing power.

In September 2001, the fastest computer cluster at ITSC and also in Hong Kong is installed. It is formed by an IBM RS/6000 SP system, three 44P-170 workstations, and an IBM 7133 disk subsystem, along with 16 SMP nodes. Each node is equipped with 4 POWER3-II processors, two 18.2 GB disks and 4 GB memory. The peak performance of the cluster is 100 GFLOPS theoretically. The cluster is used in numeric-intensive computing research projects, quantum calculations in chemical areas (calculations of NMR chemical shifts), calculations in molecular modeling , and architectural design in material science and fluid dynamics (AirFlow modeling).

Algorithms Laboratory:

A supercomputing network, with the main processor, Sparc2000/20 workstation, and 20 computing processors are linked together by an Ethernet. (http://www.cse.cuhk.edu.hk/rsh/lab/algo.html) The computer cluster is used in the area of parallel, sequential and distributed algorithm. Recent researches using the cluster include computational geometry, new algorithm for combinatorial opitmisation, and convergence and complexity aspect of several neural network learning algorithm.

SGI Visualization Research Center:

The aim of the center is to provide computational support on art visualization and application transfer through the computer cluster. (http://www.cse.cuhk.edu.hk/~crc/equip.html)

The center is facilitated with a graphics computer cluster, SGI Onyx. SGI Onyx contains a SGI INDIGO2 HIGH IMPACT workstation, along with 20 workstations.

Recent projects include real-time volume visualization in a virtual environment, interactive medical visualization, 3D texture based on animation, computer simulated bronchoscopy etc.

Numeric-Intensive Computing Server:

Numeric-Intensive Computing Server project is a joint project of the Institute of Mathematical Sciences and the Computer Services Centre at CU. A SGI Origin2000, housed in and operated by the Computer Services Centre, is available for all research staff of the University with projects that require high computation power.

The Origin2000 servers are based on the ccNUMA architecture and combine the ease-of-use of symmetric multiprocessor systems. The Origin2000 used in CU consists of 24 MIPS R10000 64-bit CPU. Each CPU provides a theoretical peak performance of 390MFLOPS. Each CPU has a built-in cache memory of 4MB. A total of 6.5 GB memory is shared among all 24 processors. The Origin 2000 system was ranked 426th in the Top500 Supercomputer List as of June 1997.

Grid Demo: Technical Session on Live Demonstrations of
Grid Technologies and Applications

Cluster 2003

December 4, 2003

Hong Kong, SAR, China

Peter Kacsuk (MTA SZTAKI Research Institute, Hungary) and Rajkumar Buyya (University of Melbourne, Australia)

Call for Papers and Presentations
(Presentation Schedule)

Recently, the Grid has been getting more and more popular not only in the academic world but even large companies like IBM, Sun and HP have expressed their strong interest in developing and providing Grid technology. A significant number of large national and international projects were launched world-wide to explore the possibilities of and advance this new technology. Large experimental scientific Grids, like physics, chemistry, biology, etc. Grids were built in the last couple of years with the hope that they will provide significant benefits for those communities in sharing and aggregating computing, storage and other resources, in achieving more intensive collaborative work and opening new ways of collaboration in the future.

Grid technology merges several important achievements of the past. Its roots came from distributed high-performance computing, parallel computing, high-throughput computing, network and Web computing. The current trends clearly show that the integration of these technologies will continue and will result in new standards in which new protocols, services and APIs for uniform access to resources, selection, and aggregation will be developed. The new efforts such as Open Grid Services Architecture (OGSA), Legion/Jxta, CPM/Jxta, and Bayanihan/.NET are expected to demonstrate this integration.

During the IEEE International Conference on Cluster Computing (Hong Kong, December 1-4) we organize a Grid Demo session. In organizing this session, we have two main important objectives. First, we want to demonstrate that Grid technology is an already feasible technology that can actively be used for the scientific and other communities. Second, by showing the results and problems of various existing Grids, we would like to provide some kind of feedback for the technology developer community to better understand what they have achieved so far and what are the significant weak links that represent serious obstacles in the usability of current Grid technology.

We invite presentations to demonstrate working Grid solutions in the following areas:

  • Grid Middleware (Information systems, job managers, brokers, monitors, accounting systems, security systems, etc.)
  • Grid Applications
  • Grid Application Development
  • Grid portals
  • Grid and Networking

The presentations will be divided into three parts. Speakers will give a talk on the main ideas of their Grid solutions in the first part (15 minutes) and give a live demonstration in the second part (10 minutes). Finally, a 5 minutes discussion time is provided.

Demonstration proposals should be submitted by October 20 as a maximum 5 page description of the demonstration. Authors will be notified on the acceptance of their proposal by October 27. The accepted presentation papers will be provided as handouts for the participants of the demo session. Demonstration proposals should be accompanied by a letter of intend explicitly declaring that at least one author will pay the Cluster'2003 conference fee and will do the demo in the technical session.

Please, send your presentation proposal to the session chairs by October 20 as well as any question concerning the organization of the Grid Demo session.

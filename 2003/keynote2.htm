Distributed Security Enforcement for
Trusted Cluster and Grid Computing

Kai Hwang
Internet and Grid Computing Laboratory
University of Southern California
Los Angeles, CA. 90089 USA

Abstract

This talk presents recent progress on distributed security architecture for trusted computing over the Internet. The GridSec project at USC is devoted to protecting pervasive, cluster, peer-to-peer, and Grid computing environments. Several research fronts are being attacked with intensive simulation, testbed construction, and NetShield software development. First, Dr. Hwang will introduce the GridSec architecture and identify related research tasks. A new security-assured resource allocation (SARA) model is presented for mapping Grid resources with high security assurance. He will outline the NetShield software approach to achieving anomaly-based intrusion detection and DDoS flood control. He will elaborate on supercomputing and business applications, international collaborations, and suggest further research challenges in Internet and Grid security.

About the speaker

Kai Hwang is a Professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science and Director of Internet and Grid Computing Laboratory at USC. He received the Ph.D. from UC Berkeley. He is the founding Editor-in-Chief of the Journal of Parallel and Distributed Computing. An IEEE Fellow, he has received numerous research grants and awards from the NSF, IBM, MIT Lincoln, Hong Kong RGC, etc. Dr. Hwang has lectured worldwide and performed advisory work for major computer industry and national laboratories. Presently, he leads a NSF-sponsored ITR Project at USC on Grid security for distributed supercomputing. Visit his personal website: http://ceng.usc.edu/~kaihwang/ for details.

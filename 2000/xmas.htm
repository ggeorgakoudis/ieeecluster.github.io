Visiting the Christmas Market of Chemnitz

Traditionally, the so-called "Chemnitzer Weihnachtsmarkt" (Christmas Market of Chemnitz) in the center of Chemnitz is opened on friday before the advent season begins (December 1st this year).
You may also have a look at this.

One day later (the saturday before the first advent, December 2nd) there's a big happening in the city: There's a parade (Bergparade) consisting of around 900 traditionally dressed miners and musicians coming from the surrounding towns and villages of Chemnitz.
This parade is a tribute to the mining industry that started in the late Middle Ages and made the region worldwide known as the "Ore Mountains".
The parade is closed by a traditional concert.

You are kindly invited to watch this parade to get a small impression about the cultural background of the region.

If you are planning to leave Chemnitz on saturday, you should stay at least until around 4 pm to not miss the parade!


Besides the parade there is the opportunity to visit some kind of "active museum" where you can watch the production of several folk-art products that are manufactured traditionally in the Ore Mountains.

On saturday there's also a cultural event in the Town Hall of Chemnitz called "Tage der Ergebirgischen Folklore", or translated "Days of folklore of the Ore Mountains". This is a traditional folk-art program with various artists from the region around Chemnitz. We also invite all conference attendees to join this event and we will arrange free admission for everybody.

At the Christmas Market itself there are many many cottages (around 170) offering original folk-art from the Ore Mountains, decoration for the christmas tree, souvenirs, things to eat and drink, and many other things.

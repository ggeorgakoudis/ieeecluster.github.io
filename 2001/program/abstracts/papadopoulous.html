Using NPACI Rocks to Easily Deploy and 
Maintain Large IA-32 based Linux Clusters

Philip M. Papadopoulos
Mason J. Katz
Greg Bruno
San Diego Supercomputer Center

 
In May of 2000, we started with 8 IA-32 compute nodes,
an Ethernet network, a 16-port Myrinet switch, and a stock
Red Hat distribution.

Over the past 18 months, we've expanded the cluster to just
over 100 IA-32 compute nodes, connected via Ethernet and a
128-port Myrinet switch, and we've enhanced Red Hat's stock
distribution to create a cluster-aware distribution we call
"NPACI Rocks."

Our cluster serves multiple roles: cluster research
development (supporting the evolution of NPACI Rocks), grid
development (connecting clusters to the Globus Grid), and
opportunistic MPI-based production code runs (GAMESS is a
heavy user).

NPACI Rocks is freely downloadable and has been used by
others to build at least 10 other clusters, some located
close by us (two 128-node clusters are being built by
the Scripps Institute of Oceanography to model ocean/air
interactions) and some in foreign countries (a 16-node
cluster at Hong Kong Baptist University).

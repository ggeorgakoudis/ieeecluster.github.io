IEEE Cluster 2001 Conference Award
October 10, 2001

Overview

Since this was the first time that a formal Industrial Exhibition was held with the IEEE Cluster Conference, the General Co-Chairs wanted to express our appreciation of companies who chose to exhibit not knowing how large the meeting would be.  Without a doubt, many interesting new products were introduced at the conference.  In essence, we wanted to identify exhibitors that brought more than just technical excellence and a recently introduced product to the conference, but also something else, which we thought unique in a light-hearted and tongue-in-cheek manner.

Selection Process

The selection process was informal.  The General Co-Chairs appointed a small (5) committee drawn from the attendees (with no commercial connections) to survey those that exhibited interesting or novel new products, one in hardware and one in software.  The award process was not meant to be exhaustive, nor an official endorsement, but merely point out an interesting and innovative product, worth a second look, and above all fun.

The Awards

The two companies selected by this committee were:

Best Commercial Exhibit – Software:

Dauger Research for introducing the Pooch cluster management tool for Apple Macintoshes. (http://daugerresearch.com/pooch)

Citation: For most innovative commercial software product exhibited at IEEE Cluster 2001 Computing Conference.
Best Commercial Exhibit – Hardware:

Dolphin for introducing the Wulfkit/Scali bundled network interface card (NIC) kit that supports a 2D/3D torus network architecture.  (http://www.dolphinics.com/aboutwulfkit.html)

Citation: For most innovative commercial hardware product exhibited at IEEE Cluster 2001 Computer Conference.

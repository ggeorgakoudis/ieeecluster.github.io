Programmability and Performance of M++ Self-Migrating Threads  

Munehiro Fukuda
fukuda@is.tsukuba.ac.jp
Naoya Suzuki 
University of Tsukuba
Luis Miguel Campos
University of California Irvine

M++ self-migrating threads are C++ compiled objects, capable of
constructing computational network and autonomously migrating
themselves over a cluster system. The main goal of M++ is to provide a
cluster computing paradigm for multi-agent simulation that has gained
popularity in artificial life and ecological simulations, and
biological modeling. This type of simulation usually requires
autonomous objects, each having its own goals and behaviors, to
interact with each other in a synthetic world. The M++ self-migrating
threads match the characteristics of such objects in multi-agent
simulation, and can thus describe them with narrower semantics gap.
Fine granularity and large scalability are two fundamental
characteristics of this type of simulations that require fast thread
context switch and migration. With the scheduling policy we have
designed, M++ manages its threads in pin-downed pages, so that
Myrinet's network interface card can directly access and transfer those
threads without unnecessary copying operations. In this paper, we
present the M++ threads' programmability and study their performance
using two multi-agent simulation programs: ant farm simulation and
growing neural network.

Scheduling parallel adaptive applications in networks of workstations and clusters of processors  

Djemai Kebbal
kebbal@lifl.fr
E. G. Talbi 
J. M. Geib 
Université des Sciences et Technologies de Lille

This paper presents a dynamic multi-application scheduling approach for
running and scheduling parallel adaptive applications in Networks of
Workstations (NOWs) and Clusters of Processors (COPs). Parallel
adaptive applications have the property of varying their parallelism
degree dynamically following availability of resources and changes in
the underlying environment's state. In our model, each parallel
adaptive application has its own scheduler responsible for optimizing
resources which it uses. Multi-application scheduling consists of
sharing resources among applications fairly and uses a combined
(time-sharing and space-sharing) scheduling approach. The scheduling
approach is characterized especially by dynamic arrivals of
applications, remapping of allocation in order to handle dynamic
arrivals and departures of applications and the underlying environment
changes. Therefore, resources are fairly shared among applications. The
multi-application scheduler interacts with application schedulers in
order to optimize the scheduling approach. Application schedulers are
responsible for applying multi-application scheduling decisions and
optimizing internally resources exploited by the application. Beyond
the adaptive aspect which allows parallel applications to exploit idle
cycles in NOWs with respect to the personal character of workstations,
the proposed model provides a multi-application scheduling support
which ensures better resource exploitation globally and fair resource
sharing between parallel applications.

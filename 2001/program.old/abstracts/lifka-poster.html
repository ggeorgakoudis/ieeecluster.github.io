High Performance Computing with Microsoft Windows 2000  

David Lifka
Cornell Theory Center

The Cornell Theory Center has moved its entire
computing infrastructure over to Dell Servers running
Microsoft Windows 2000. This change has led to significant
improvements in performance, reliability and TCO over the
proprietary UNIX-based systems CTC operated in the past.
We will provide an overview of the current CTC systems,
examples of applications that have benefited, and discuss
how reliability and TCO have been affected by high volume,
industry standard hardware and software.

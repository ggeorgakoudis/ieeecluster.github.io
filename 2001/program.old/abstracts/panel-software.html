Panel: 2001: A Software Odyssey 

Moderator:
Pete Beckman

Participants:
Bernard Tourancheau
Putchong Uthayopas
Donald Becker
Al Geist
William Gropp

Since the first black monolith for cluster software appeared in the early 
90s, heralding networks of workstations as a parallel computing platform, 
software has been at the very core of the evolution of cluster 
computing. From the very first job spawning mechanisms and TCP/IP-based 
message passing to today's batch schedulers and zero-copy message layers, 
how much has changed?

What are the important challenges, and how are they being addressed?

The distinguished panelists, all with a long history in cluster enabling 
software, will focus their discussions on several points:

  • How did we get here, and why has it taken so long?
  • Has evolution stopped? Are mass extinctions beginning? What 
    technologies are doomed to be a anthropological footnote in cluster 
    computing history?
  • What cluster software traits, concepts and implementations are most 
    successful. Why?
  • Where does cluster software remain primitive and Neanderthal? What are 
    the fundamentally hard problems?
  • When, where, and why will the next black monolith appear? When can we 
    expect a profound shift in capability for cluster software, matching the 
    impact of the transistor, Unix, or message passing?

Please join the panelists as they answer these questions and then discuss 
the successes and failures and new breakthroughs expected for cluster software.

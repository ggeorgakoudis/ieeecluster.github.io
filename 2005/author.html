Instructions for Submitting Camera Ready Final Papers/Posters to Cluster 2005

Due dates

Full papers in the main conference and the heteropar workshop are due on Friday, July 30. 
Abstracts/extended abstracts for the posters are due on Monday, August 15.

Instructions for Submission of Final Papers/Posters

For reviewed papers in the main conference track upload your final paper through the submission/review/publication website using you submission passcode. Proceed in the same way for reviewed submissions that were accepted as posters.

For the workshop track and the newly submitted posters please go to our submission website and fill out the information and select the appropriate submission category. This will accept your paper and create an account.

Formatting Information

Your paper should be formatted according to the IEEE Computing Society Proceedings format. You can find the templates and description of this format here. In particular there are instructions, layout and sample templates in the FTP archive of CS press:
  • Author guideline as plain text (ftp, http).
  • Layout template in PDF (ftp, http), PS (ftp, http) or MSWord (ftp, http).
  • Sample document with instructions in PDF (ftp, http) or MSWord (ftp, http).
  • Latex package with instructions (ftp, http) macros and formats in one zip file (ftp, http). The latex8.sty/latex8.bst format should comply with with the required layout.
Please submit your paper as a fully formatted PDF document, with all special fonts except for the standard 13 fonts included (standard fonts are Times Roman, New Times Roman, Courier, Helvetica, Arial...). Please quickly check the document info in all the free acrobat readers under Manu:File:Documents Properties:Fonts. If you are unable to print or distill into PDF you can alternatively submit a printer file in Postscript or a source file in MS Word or LaTeX. If you are submitting a source format make sure it is self contained and includes all fonts and macros (in a zip file).

Page limits

Your final camera ready original for full papers and workshop papers should be no more than 10 pages long. There will be a per-page charge for papers that go over 10 pages in length as follows: $50.00 for the first extra page, and $100.00 for each additional extra page. This charge is to be paid as part of the registration fee for the conference (there will be a space on the registration form for this fee).

For posters you can either submit a single page abstract or a two page extended abstract formatted with the same style than the full papers. Posters are limited to 2 pages and there are no option to buy extra pages.

Note that, as stated on the IEEE web page, the main body of the text should be single-spaced, two columns, and in 10-point font. This is a very compact format, so your original drafts submitted for review will shrink a lot in size.
